T. M. Bainter

Hey, I'm Tylan Bainter!

In 2017, I began to recreate some of my favorite video game mechanics in Epic Games'  3D computer graphics game engine called Unreal Engine. Within the following five years, I built many projects the engine's various systems (Blueprint, Niagara, UMG) in conjunction with Blender and Gimp. These projects helped me to learn the inner workings of the engine, the fundamentals and intricacies of programming, and without even knowing it, the structure of front-end web development.

During these years, I built a multitude of projects including a space-themed shooter for mobile platforms, a one-shot dungeon-crawling RPG featuring my first "boss fight", and chiefly, a fan remake of the 2006 Nintendo DS game Rune Factory.

Eventually, I was curious enough to try my hand at web development close to the end of 2022. I was pleasantly surprised to realize that front-end web development was structured in a fashion very similar to systems I'd already been using in Unreal such as UMG (Unreal Motion Graphics). Having this prior experience allowed me to move quickly into the practice; in fact, what you're reading now is my fifth and final certification project for freeCodeCamp's "Responsive Web Design" course.
